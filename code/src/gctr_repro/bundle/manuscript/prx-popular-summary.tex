\section*{Popular Summary}

Variational quantum algorithms depend on a classical optimizer, and choosing
that optimizer can consume many expensive circuit evaluations.  We test
whether simple graph measurements can route each problem to a better
fixed-budget optimization strategy while a statistical gate rejects uncertain
recommendations.  The study separates model development, calibration, and a
locked audit across four synthetic graph families.  The router slightly lowers
average anytime regret relative to a strong family-level fallback, but it
accepts only twelve of 160 recommendations, four accepted routes are harmful,
and its prespecified coverage requirement fails.  The method therefore does
not earn a deployment claim.  Its broader contribution is an auditable way to
separate average improvement, selective reliability, conditional failures,
and the offline and online costs of optimizer routing before such methods are
tested on quantum hardware.
