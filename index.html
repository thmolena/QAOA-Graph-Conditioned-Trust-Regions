Reliability Auditing for Structural Routing of QAOA Optimizers

Molena Huynh

Preprint · 2026

A learned router can lower average QAOA regret while harming the few instances on which it intervenes. This project compares nine optimizer arms under the same target‑free 32‑call ceiling, routes from invariant graph summaries, and abstains to a strong family‑matched control. The observed mean improves slightly, but the frozen reliability gate fails: only 12 of 160 graphs are routed and four accepted routes are harmful.

A Target‑Free Fixed‑Budget Test

Depth‑\(p\) QAOA prepares \(|\gamma,\beta\rangle\) from a cost Hamiltonian \(C\) and mixer \(B\), and a classical loop maximizes \[ F_G(\theta)=\langle\theta|C|\theta\rangle, \qquad \theta\in\mathbb{R}^{2p}. \] On hardware, this objective is estimated from Born‑rule samples rather than read out directly, so every optimizer query costs repeated state preparation, execution, and measurement.

Every arm receives exactly 32 objective calls and records best‑so‑far quality at calls 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, and 32. The primary score is equal‑weight area under normalized checkpoint regret (AURC), with exact MaxCut used only after optimization for small‑instance analysis. No target or exact optimum controls stopping. All seeds and signed SPSA evaluations are counted.

Structural Routing with a Matched Fallback

Sixteen relabelling‑invariant summaries encode graph size, density, degree distribution, clustering, triangles, and Laplacian spectrum. A low‑capacity multi‑output ridge model fitted on development graphs predicts the AURC of nine arms, \[ s(x)=\arg\min_m \widehat u_m(x). \] The portfolio contains concentration, four fixed TQA schedules, nearest neighbour transfer, random multistart, SPSA, and the legacy graph‑conditioned trust‑region policy.

For each synthetic graph family, the arm with lowest development AURC is frozen as fallback \(b(f)\). A separate 48‑graph calibration split supplies an upper residual order statistic, \[ U(x)=\widehat u_{s(x)}(x)-\widehat u_{b(f)}(x)+q. \] Routing occurs only when \(s(x)\neq b(f)\) and \(U(x)<0\); otherwise the family fallback runs. Fixed quotas and sequential cross‑split de‑duplication prevent exact exchangeability, so this is a conformal‑motivated abstention heuristic, not a finite‑sample coverage certificate.

Internal Locking Is Not External Preregistration

The runner hashes the graph protocol, implementation, non‑audit traces, trained legacy model, selector, calibration threshold, decision, and outcomes. It writes the decision before evaluating audit outcomes and refuses to overwrite an existing audit directory. Those controls provide tamper evidence and enforce execution order.

They do not provide an independent timestamp: protocol, frozen decision, and audit outcomes first entered public Git history together. The current study is therefore described as internally locked, not externally preregistered. A future confirmatory run must publish the executable protocol and trained decision rule to an immutable registry before generating any audit outcome.

The Mean Improves, but the Reliability Gate Fails

The audit contains 160 new \(p=2\), \(n\in\{12,14\}\) MaxCut graphs, balanced across four generator families and two sizes. The global TQA schedule has mean AURC 0.17277, the family‑matched fallback 0.17207, and the abstaining router 0.16930. The paired mean difference is \(-0.00277\), but its locked percentile endpoint is close to zero and is not robust to reasonable alternative inference.

Twelve Interventions, Four Harms

The router accepts only \(12/160\) proposed departures from the family control. Four are harmful, all in the Barabási‑Albert family; random‑regular graphs supply most of the aggregate gain. Descriptive coverage is \(141/160=0.88125\), below the frozen 0.90 gate. The mechanical decision is therefore no‑go even though the observed joint harm rate is only \(4/160=0.025\).

Marginal Harm Is Not Accepted‑Route Safety

Under an explicitly hypothetical iid‑binomial model, observing at most 141 covered graphs has probability 0.249 when true coverage is 0.90. The point miss is therefore compatible with nominal variation. One‑sided 95% exact upper bounds are 0.169 for coverage failure, 0.056 for joint harm, and 0.609 for harm conditional on acceptance. The locked design is not iid, so these are sensitivity diagnostics, not formal risk certificates.

The Legacy Learned Policy Is a Negative Control

The legacy graph‑conditioned trust‑region arm records mean AURC 0.28577. Fixed TQA schedules at \(d_t=0.75\) and \(d_t=0.50\) record 0.17277 and 0.17381. Concentration, random multistart, and SPSA are also weaker in this exact low‑depth regime. The study therefore does not claim that learned angle geometry is an operational advantage.

Reproducible Research Package

The repository root is the single installable source for the specops-gctr distribution. It contains fixed‑budget arm runners, exact query accounting, protocol and decision hashing, invariant structural features, ridge routing, abstention, manifest validation, the legacy GCTR implementation, and exact risk diagnostics. The 160‑graph evidence directory is immutable to the runner. The design‑only v2 specification is unregistered and unrun; it contains no future outcome. 

python -m pip install "specops-gctr @ git+https://github.com/thmolena/QAOA-Graph-Conditioned-Trust-Regions.git"
gctr-optimize --family er --n 10 --budget 100 --json

# Audit validation and sensitivity diagnostics require a checkout:
gctr-portfolio --config configs/portfolio_heterogeneous_confirmatory.json \
  --output-dir portfolio_results/heterogeneous_confirmatory_v1 --validate-only
